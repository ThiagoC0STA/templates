### Two paths

Open-source models offer flexibility and control; proprietary ones often lead on performance and support. The two paths differ in:

- Where and how you can run the model.
- Cost, support, and update cycles.
- Control over fine-tuning and data.

The right choice depends on your constraints.

### When to consider open source

Open-source vs proprietary AI: a practical guide starts with when open source makes sense:

- You need to run on-prem or in strict environments.
- You want to fine-tune or extend the model.
- Cost and vendor independence matter.

### When proprietary can win

Proprietary options can be the better fit when:

- You need the best possible quality with minimal engineering.
- You want managed updates and SLAs.
- Time to market is critical.

Evaluate both for each use case rather than adopting one strategy everywhere.

### Conclusion

There is no single right answer between open-source and proprietary AI. Match the model and vendor to the use case, and be ready to revisit as your needs and the market evolve.
