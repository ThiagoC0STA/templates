### Human-first AI UX

AI-powered products succeed when they feel helpful and predictable, not opaque or robotic. Human-first AI UX means prioritizing:

- Clarity about what the system can and cannot do.
- User control and recovery from errors.
- Predictable, understandable behavior.

Design for clarity, control, and recovery from errors.

### Principles that help

Designing AI-driven user experiences that feel human starts with a few principles:

- Show what the system is doing and what it can and can’t do.
- Give users ways to correct or override outputs.
- Use language and patterns that match user mental models.

When in doubt, prioritize clarity and control over cleverness.

### Test with real users

Simulate and test edge cases and failures. To test with real users effectively:

- Simulate edge cases and failure modes.
- Run tests with representative users.
- Iterate until users feel in control.

The best AI experiences are those where users still feel in control.

### Conclusion

AI-driven experiences that feel human are within reach. Focus on transparency, control, and real-user feedback, and your product will stand out for the right reasons.
