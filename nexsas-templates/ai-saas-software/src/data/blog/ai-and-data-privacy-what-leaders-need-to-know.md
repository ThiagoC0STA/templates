### Privacy and AI

AI systems often rely on large datasets. Leaders need to understand how data is collected, used, and retained. Key questions to answer include:

- Where does training and inference data come from?
- How long is data retained and who can access it?
- How do you stay compliant with evolving regulations?

Getting clarity on these protects both your customers and your business.

### Key areas

AI and data privacy: what leaders need to know centers on:

- Data minimization and purpose limitation.
- Consent and transparency with users.
- Vendor and model governance.

Getting these right protects both your customers and your business.

### Practical steps

Audit your AI use cases, update policies, and ensure engineering and legal work together. Practical steps include:

- Auditing AI use cases for data and compliance risk.
- Updating policies and notices for AI-driven processing.
- Aligning engineering and legal from the start.

### Conclusion

Privacy and AI can coexist when leaders prioritize both. Make data governance part of your AI strategy from day one.
